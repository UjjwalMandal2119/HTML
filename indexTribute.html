A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

1931-2015

The Missile man of India

Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam, known as the Missile Man of India, made remarkable contributions to India's defense and space programs. Here are ten key points about his life and achievements: Early Life – Born on 15 October 1931 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, Kalam came from a humble background and worked hard to support his education. Education – He studied aerospace engineering at the Madras Institute of Technology, where he developed a keen interest in aviation and missile technology. ISRO Contributions – He played a crucial role in developing India’s first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III), which successfully deployed the Rohini satellite in 1980. Missile Development – As the chief architect of the Integrated Guided Missile Development Program (IGMDP), he led the development of strategic missiles like Agni and Prithvi, strengthening India's defense capabilities. Pokhran-II Nuclear Tests – He was a key figure in India’s 1998 nuclear tests, which established India as a nuclear-armed state. Presidency – Served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007, earning the title of "People’s President" for his accessibility and vision. Awards & Honors – He received India’s highest civilian honor, the Bharat Ratna, along with the Padma Bhushan and Padma Vibhushan. Vision for India – He authored books like India 2020, advocating for India’s transformation into a developed nation. Youth Inspiration – He was deeply committed to motivating students, often engaging in lectures and discussions to inspire innovation and scientific thinking. Legacy – His contributions continue to influence India's scientific and defense advancements, and his birthday, 15 October, is celebrated as World Students' Day.

Biographies

  • India 2020: A Vision for the New Millennium (1998) – Outlines a roadmap for India to become a developed nation.
  • Wings of Fire: An Autobiography (1999) – Chronicles his journey from humble beginnings to becoming the President of India.
  • Ignited Minds: Unleashing the Power Within India (2002) – Inspires young minds to achieve greatness.
  • The Luminous Sparks (2004) – A collection of thoughts, anecdotes, and poems.
  • Mission India: A Vision for Indian Youth (2005) – Encourages the youth to take action in nation-building.
  • You Are Born to Blossom: Take My Journey Beyond (2010) – Focuses on educational reforms.
